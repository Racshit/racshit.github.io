About Me

After 3 years of being a good corporate employee, I have decided with unwavering doubt that I will put on my shoe of being an academic weapon, yet again. My significant contribution in the technical space has now kindled a desire of challenging myself in the management field. The Aston MBA course along with the flexibility of choosing an optional module seems to align seamlessly with my career goal of leading businesses to excellence. Being a single child moving to a completely new city after every 2 years due to a military background, has made me the adaptable and socially sound person that I am today. My extroverted nature seems a great requisite to be able to make full use of the Aston Edge personal development programme. I have always been involved in extra responsibilities since my college years which are very evident by completing the 3 internships, all in different Computer Science fields even before I started working full-time. My current workplace also showed their appreciation by awarding me with a Gold Medal for a critical project I was involved in. Other than academics, I was also the coordinator of Publicity and Marketing International Committee which gave me an opportunity to interact with people from different countries around the world. I was also a member of my college’s Music Club for an unusual art form of beatboxing. Since my work started, I had always been involved in developing training guides and giving Knowledge Transfer sessions to new-joiners and even colleagues from different teams. My manager saw this ability of mine and gave me an opportunity to take interviews of candidates from the APAC and US region I would want to apply the same ‘learn and implement’ technique that I did for my technical aspirations to my management interest..
